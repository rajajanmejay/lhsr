About the Lab

Decades of Hypersonic Excellence

The Laboratory for Hypersonic and Shock Wave Research (LHSR) at IISc Bengaluru is a world-leading facility dedicated to the study of high-speed flows, shock waves, and hypersonic aerodynamics.

Home to India's first hypersonic shock tunnel (1971), LHSR has been at the forefront of aerospace research for over five decades. Our facilities enable ground-testing of vehicles designed to fly faster than Mach 5 — bridging the gap between computational models and real flight conditions.

Our interdisciplinary team works on aerodynamic design, combustion chemistry, plasma physics, materials science, and biomedical engineering applications of shock waves.

// Research

What We Study

Fundamental and applied research at the intersection of high-speed aerodynamics, shock physics, and aerospace engineering.

01
Hypersonic Aerodynamics
Experimental and computational studies of vehicles at Mach 5+: shock–boundary layer interactions, aerodynamic heating, high-speed inlet design, and re-entry vehicle aerodynamics.
SBLI, Scramjet Intakes, Aerodynamic Heating, Re-entry
02
Hypersonic Aeroelasticity
Fluid–structure interactions in high-speed flows: panel flutter, shock-induced deformation, and aero-thermo-elastic coupling for hypersonic vehicle design.
Panel Flutter, Thermoelasticity, FSI
03
High-Enthalpy Flows
Real-gas effects, thermal non-equilibrium, dissociation, ionization, and radiation in hypersonic and re-entry environments up to 10 MJ/kg specific enthalpy.
Real-Gas, Non-equilibrium, Radiation
04
Shockwave Physics
Generation, propagation, reflection, and interaction of shock waves. Visualized using Schlieren and shadowgraph optics in shock tubes and free-piston tunnels.
Shock Reflection, Schlieren, Shock Focusing
05
High Temperature Chemical Kinetics
High-temperature reaction rates, ignition delays, and species evolution using shock tubes, flow reactors, and spectroscopic diagnostics for combustion applications.
Ignition Delays, Spectroscopy, Flow Reactor
06
Biological Engineering
Shock-wave-based biomedical applications: extracorporeal shock-wave therapy, targeted drug delivery, cell manipulation, and sterilisation using controlled blast waves.
ESWT, Drug Delivery, Cell Manipulation
07
Materials
Thermal protection systems and structural materials under extreme shock and thermal loading. Characterisation of TPS coatings, HCP metals, and high strain-rate failure.
TPS Coatings, HCP Metals, Shock Loading
08
Blast-wave Physics
Explosion dynamics, blast wave propagation, and interaction with structures, fluids, and biological systems. Applications in safety, defence, and blast-injury mitigation.
Explosion Dynamics, Blast Loading, Structural Response
09
Industrial Applications
Translating shock physics to aerospace wind-tunnel testing, ISRO/DRDO collaborations, supercritical CO₂ systems, energy technology, and industrial processing.
ISRO, DRDO, Aerospace Testing

// Opportunities

Join the Lab

We are always looking for motivated researchers and engineers to join our team at all levels.

🎓
PhD Positions
We admit PhD students through the IISc admissions process. Students with backgrounds in aerospace, mechanical, chemical engineering, or physics are encouraged to apply.
📐
M.Tech Research
M.Tech (Research) positions for students seeking deep experimental and computational skills in high-speed flows — instrumentation, visualization, and data analysis.
🔭
Post-Doctoral Fellows
Post-doctoral opportunities for candidates with a PhD in related fields. Strong experimental skills in shock tunnel instrumentation, optical diagnostics, or CFD validation preferred.
🛠
Project Associates
Funded project associate positions periodically available for B.Tech/M.Tech candidates. These roles involve hands-on experimental work and facility operations.
🌍
Visiting Researchers
We welcome visiting researchers, interns from premier institutes, and international collaborators for short-term stays. Funding may be available through SERB, CSIR, or bilateral programs.
🤝
Industry Collaboration
LHSR collaborates with ISRO, DRDO, NAL, and aerospace industries for sponsored research and testing. Organizations seeking hypersonic testing or consulting are welcome to contact us.
What We Look For
  • Strong fundamentals in fluid mechanics and thermodynamics
  • Background in aerospace, mechanical, chemical engineering, or physics
  • Enthusiasm for experimental research and hands-on lab work
  • Comfort with data analysis, instrumentation, and uncertainty quantification
  • Ability to work in a collaborative, multidisciplinary environment
  • Programming skills (Python, MATLAB, or equivalent) are advantageous

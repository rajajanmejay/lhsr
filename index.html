01 — About

Pushing the Boundaries
of High-Speed Flight

The Laboratory for Hypersonic and Shock Wave Research (LHSR) at the Indian Institute of Science is one of the foremost experimental aerodynamics research centers in Asia.

Our research spans hypersonic aerodynamics, scramjet propulsion, shock-boundary layer interactions, chemical kinetics, detonation physics, and high-enthalpy gas dynamics — addressing fundamental and applied problems relevant to next-generation aerospace vehicles.

The lab operates a unique collection of impulse facilities capable of reproducing extreme conditions encountered during hypersonic flight, enabling ground-based testing at Mach 4 to Mach 15 with enthalpies up to 25 MJ/kg.

Research Areas

Hypersonic aerodynamics · Scramjet intakes · Shock-boundary layer interaction · Chemical kinetics · Detonation · High-speed combustion · Bio-inspired engineering · Thermal barrier coatings

Key Milestone

HST-1 was India's first Hypersonic Shock Tunnel, establishing LHSR as a national pioneer in high-speed experimental research.

National Impact

Collaborates with ISRO, DRDO, HAL and leading international universities in support of national hypersonic and space vehicle programs.
